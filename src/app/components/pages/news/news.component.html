CS_AIW Cluster and the EU efforts to fight Cancer

The Commission has set an overall goal of improving the lives of more than 3 million people by 2030. The Cancer Survivorship – AI for Well-being cluster is helping for the realization of this objective to be realized. The cluster brings together 11 EU-funded projects working in Artificial Intelligence for healthcare and well-being. Primarily, the Cluster focuses in the post-cancer treatment space with a collective aim to cross-fertilise. This brings to transcend individual project experiences for the wider good and outcome of their individual projects. The Cluster started in October 2020 and it is evolving. Presently, it is flourishing with members collectively working on related issues spanning topics around clinical, technical and business. The Cluster fosters cooperation for the ultimate benefit of the patients, care-givers and of course, medical professionals involved in the care team undertaking.   • September 2023

Linking in with related Projects - INCLUDING

How does the PEERS project engage with INCLUDING? Have a read of the article published in the INCLUDING project. Interoperability is a vital part of the PEERS project and also for INCLUDING. Both the INCLUDING and PEERS projects are inter-related by their mission and quest to strengthen European resilience, although the scope of PEERS is wider as it covers the full spectrum of CBRNe and not only the nuclear and radiological threat aspects. Notwithstanding that, Article 6 of the European Commission Action Plan between Civil, Space and Defense Industry, 2021 is extremely prevalent to both projects and in mutual support the development of Better Practice Guides and APIs to strengthen interoperability, will further help to contribute to the sharing of knowledge and resources as well as the long-term sustainability intent directed by the Co-Creation Group in PEERS. Through the integrated Virtual Reality component of the PEERS ecosystem, the learning capacity of INCLUDING can be strengthened for the wider benefit of the community supported by both projects. PEERS is committed to holding more Meetings of Minds (MoM) events and sees a logical connection in obtaining further co-creation feedback so that the gamma version of the PEERS platform delivers what the community needs and complements the federated model of INCLUDING.
